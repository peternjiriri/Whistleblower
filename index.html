Introduction

The Code of Corporate Governance Practices for Issuers of Securities to the Public, 2015 requires publicly listed companies to provide a platform for the disclosure of serious malpractices. LBDA whistle blow anonymous provides a platform where all stakeholders (employees, shareholders, customers, suppliers and the general public) can report on serious malpractices in a safe, confidential and secure way.

What can be reported?

The following are the qualifying disclosures under the LBDA Whistleblowing Policy:

  • Violations of law;
  • Gross Waste-Misappropriation of company resources;
  • Abuse of authority;
  • Fraud;
  • Corruption;
  • Misconduct-Conduct contrary to LBDA’s ethical principles and values;
  • Collusive practices;
  • System breaches;
  • Any other activity that undermines the company’s operations/endangers employees’ health and safety;
  • The cover up of any of these.

Personal or general complaints shall be dealt with through the appropriate Human Resource and other established mechanisms within the company.

Who is responsible for whistle-blowing and the protection of whistle blowers?

LBDA has a designated function responsible for acting on whistle blows and the protection of whistle-blowers. Information in respect of the number of whistle blows received and the number investigated will be reported at least annually.

What happens after I file a report with the LBDA whistle blower?

Once the disclosure is received, it will be assessed and where necessary, an investigation will be launched by the Group CEO’s office. A report on the findings will be presented and in consultation with the board, the appropriate action will be taken. Where the whistle blower wishes to know the feedback on the outcome of the investigation, a follow up is done to update them.

Will I suffer any detriment for making a disclosure?

A stakeholder will not suffer detriment because of whistleblowing. However, while protection is provided under the whistle blowing policy, false and malicious disclosures will not be tolerated.

Is the confidentiality for the whistle blower guaranteed?

All whistle-blower disclosures will be treated in the strictest confidence and all reasonable steps will be taken to protect the identity of the whistle-blower. If a whistle-blower chooses to remain anonymous, it is important to ensure that enough information is provided in the disclosure to facilitate a thorough investigation.
